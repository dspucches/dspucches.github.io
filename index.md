# About Me

I have always had a passion for computers, especially breaking them "accidently" and having to fix them before my parents lost their mind. For me, it was to see how I could speed this computer up to play my video games with higher efficiency. Eventually, I coerced my parents into funding my first PC build, I believe I was 14/15 at the time and it was glorious! I enjoyed holding all the different chipsets, fascinated by their do-dads, resistors, and the gold buses'. I enjoyed the piecing together of the hardware as if it was a highly expensive lego set. From there I just played my video games, messed with the command prompt but that was it. My education on the computer system took a hault, but I still felt a pull toward this technology.

Fast forward to 2018 where I completed my A.A.S. in Computer Information Systems: System and Network Administration with a local community college. It was tough for me, since I lacked the discipline to really absorb the information, "C's get degrees" mindset. In 2021, when I returned from my third rotation overseas, I started my Bachlors in Cybersecurity. This was it, this was my "I'm getting out of my currently field so I can actually spend my days working on projects that I enjoy" moment. During my first year attending college, I took the time to complete my CompTIA Security+ certification. All of this while working full-time, in college full-time as well. Let's just say, it was rough, but I got it done. That Spetember I entered the Information Technology field and that following year (2022) I completed my CySA+ while in the same boat as before, also rough.

As previously mentioned, I have been working within the Information Technology (IT) Field since September of 2021. Where I worked for a Managed Service Provider (MSP) providing IT services to many different types of clients. Some of these services included network infrastructure upgrades, Office 365 administration, firewall conifgurations, managing workstations, and much more. At this MSP, I solved a year long networking problem one of our clients were having, that felt like a great accomplishment. Following that position, I left to work as a Jr. Network Engineer with another MSP, focusing on level 2/3 tickets and troubleshooting client network issues with different systems. Unfortunately, I had to leave this position when I was offered my first Cybersecurity gig. It was a tough decision, I had two great Sr. Network Engineers to learn from, with a vast amount of knowledge and experience, but I did not want to pass up the opportunity to get my foot in the door.
