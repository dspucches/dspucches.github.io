# How to start my Cybersecurity career?

<hr>

Asking the right questions in any environment will aide in developing 99% of skillsets you want to achieve. A big question that many individuals looking to make a career change may ask is "How do I start my Cybersecurity career?". For some, it may seem quite intimidating, there is such a plethora of information scattered among the world wide web that you can indulge yourself into. When individuals hear "cybersecurity" they think of a guy wearing a black hoodie, running commands on an old, unix looking terminal, reading information propogating on the screen like it was a clip from The Matrix. Well, even though understanding how a terminal/command prompt works, and how to push commands through it to tell the machine what you want to do is good to know, it not what makes cybersecurity, cybersecurity.

There are many different avenues of approach within the cyber field. Just like getting into Information Technology (IT) in general, you can either be a System Administrator, Network Administrator, Database Admin, etc etc; there are so many fields within IT, and cybersecurity is one of them. When we introduce cybersecurity to the mix, there are many different areas of expertise within the cybersecurity field of IT. There are two "main" categories in my opinion and that would be Blue Team, if you like to defend and Red Team, if you like to attack. Blue teaming could be working for a Security Operations Center (SOC) or conducting vulnerability management on information systems, creating policies, threat hunting, bug bounties, the options are almost endless. As for Red Teaming, you can be a physical penetration tester, breaking into businesses and testing their physical security or you can the hacker behind a terminal using modern, GUI assisted tools to help you find vulnerabilities and weaknesses in an information system, and maybe exploiting them if it is within your designated scope.
